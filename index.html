Wild Heart Sancturay

Non-Profit Redesign

At-a-Glance


Wild Heart Sanctuary (WHS) required assistance with a website redesign to better the user experience and to streamline the donation flow.



Collaborators: Sergey Khrushchev, Che Schumacher, Christian Weidle

Tools: Figma, Mural, Mirro, Photoshop

Duration: Four weeks

Project Overview.

The Problem:

Wildlife sanctuary has a confusing and outdated website that leads users to abandon the website prematurely and as a result the donation flow suffers.

The Solution:

We implemented Design Thinking methodology to reach the optimal solution for this project.

User Research Stage

Research Plan.

  • User testing was done to familiarize ourselves with the current site and menu navigation.
  • A card sorting activity was also used to evaluate information architecture of the website
  • A website brand audit was conducted to assess the current direction of the website.
  • Lastly a interview with the founder of Wild Heart Sanctuary

User Persona & StoryBoard.

Lauren Diaz represents an ideal supporter for Wild-Heart Sanctuary:

  • Lauren loves horses more than people
  • Lauren appreciates the mission of Wild Heart Sanctuary
  • Lauren is looking to donate her extra cash $$$ to a worthy cause

Ideation Stage

Industry Analysis.

We looked around at other big name animal non profits to get a better understanding of what trends were popular and to compare what features other non-profits have included in their designs. Comparing Wild Heart Sanctuary to other similar non-profits opened our eyes to what other possible ways there are to complete our goal.

User flow

A new and simplified user flow for all those looking to donate through the website. The CTA being donate right on the landing page.


Prioritization matrix.

By forming an idea prioritization matrix it allowed us to figure out where our focus should be. After narrowing down these priorities, our next steps were straightforward.

  1. Clean Website Design(New branding)
  2. Add Venmo Donate Option
  3. Highlight Festival or Fairs at The Sanctuary

Design Stage

Paper First.

Making paper mockups allowed for quick iterations of changes before we committed to a digital low-fi design. Our key screens being:

Homepage - wanting the visitor, to feel immersed in the beauty of the wild horses and the

Donation screen - This is where we wanted to streamline the process into a simple and intuitive, ‘one scroll that’s all’ process.

New Branding Opportunity.

The original website did not match up with the brand voice they wish to portray, it feel flat, one dimensional, colorless and lacked direction. We asked Sonya, during the interview, to describe Wild Heart as they see it in 3-4 adjectives. Their answers were:

  • Whimsical
  • Joyful
  • Heartfelt
  • Nature
  • Wild
  • Blissful

From there, we were able to pick the colors and fonts that conveyed this thematically and create a style guide
